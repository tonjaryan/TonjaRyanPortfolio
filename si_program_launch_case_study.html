The challenge & approach

01 / 04

Each fall, the Sanger Learning Center launches a new cohort of SI Leaders — peer educators who run weekly sessions for 2,500+ students in historically difficult STEM courses across 13 undergraduate colleges. By spring 2025, three things needed to be true at once: the training curriculum had to be redesigned (returning leaders found it repetitive, new leaders under-prepared), a layer of admin and compliance content had to be added (Canvas, hours logging, safety, mandated reporter), and the operational rollout had to actually run — room reservations, faculty introductions, Canvas pages, leader assignments.

This isn't three separate projects. The curriculum decisions shape the training schedule; the training schedule shapes the room reservation timing; the room reservations shape the leader-to-room assignments; the leader assignments depend on cohort-time data from the registrar that doesn't exist until late summer. It's one project with sequencing constraints across a four-month window.

I led the work end-to-end alongside the SI supervisor team: stakeholder discovery, three-track curriculum design, materials and a parallel virtual onboarding build, then the operational rollout — 140+ room reservations coordinated with the registrar, Canvas page setup for 70+ leaders, cohort time mapping against student schedules, and the late-summer email cycle pairing each leader with their faculty partner.

Three training tracks

02 / 04

The biggest design insight from discovery: one training format for all leaders had been failing two audiences simultaneously. New leaders needed slower-paced foundational work; returning leaders needed depth, refresh, and the community side. The fix was three distinct sessions with deliberate audience segmentation — each running on its own day with its own facilitator guide.

Track 01

New Leader Training

First-time SI Leaders

Intensive foundational onboarding. What SI is, why it works, the three pillars, facilitation techniques, and the lesson-planning rhythm. Paced for leaders who've never run a session before.

Format
  • In-person · Half-day
  • Scenario practice + role play
  • Paired with virtual module
Track 02

All-Leader Virtual

All SI Leaders (new + returning)

Synchronous virtual session covering the new admin layer — Canvas, hours logging, safety/compliance, mandated reporter — that didn't exist in the previous curriculum.

Format
  • Zoom · Synchronous
  • Recorded for async access
  • Compliance attestation
Track 03

All-Leader In-Person

All SI Leaders (new + returning)

Community day. Cross-cohort connection, advanced facilitation workshops, problem-solving on real session challenges, and meet-your-supervisor time. The piece returning leaders consistently said was most valuable.

Format
  • In-person · Half-day
  • Workshop + community
  • Supervisor 1:1 windows

Operational rollout

03 / 04

Curriculum is the front-stage work. Operations is what makes the curriculum land. If a leader walks into week one without a room, a Canvas page, or a faculty contact, the program has failed them — no matter how good the training was. Five operational workstreams ran in parallel through June–August, each with its own dependencies and sequencing.

The virtual module

04 / 04

Discovery surfaced that a lot of the core SI content was being delivered live but didn't need to be — leaders said they'd rather work through the foundational material on their own time and use live training for the parts that genuinely benefit from being in a room together. I built the foundational curriculum as a fully interactive self-paced module that leaders complete before the in-person session.

That freed up the live time for what only works live: scenario practice, community-building, supervisor connection, and the nuanced conversations that don't happen async. It also gave each leader a working draft of their first lesson plan, professor email, and class pitch before walking into the in-person session.

SI Leader Onboarding · Interactive Web Module

Self-paced asynchronous training built as a single-page web app. Auto-saves progress, generates a personalized handoff document, and gives every leader a draft of their first lesson plan, professor email, and class pitch before they walk into the in-person session.
