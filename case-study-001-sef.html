The challenge & approach

01 / 05

The Student Educator Forum hadn't taken place since February 2022. The Sanger Learning Center wanted to relaunch SEF as the central hub for training and professionalizing the hundreds of student employees who tutor, mentor, coach, and facilitate learning across UT Austin.

We were running it as a deliberate soft launch — late spring, mid-April, colliding with finals and end-of-year burnout. Internal target was modest: ~40 attendees, primarily SLC's own student educators with strategic invitations to a handful of campus partners. The bar was "prove the model can run again" before scaling up.

There was no recent playbook. We rebuilt the operational scaffolding — registration, run-of-show, moderator training, breakout-room logistics, post-event measurement — from a near-blank slate, in twelve weeks, with a committee of seven splitting work across four functions. My partner Ángeles and I owned the connective tissue: the Zoom environment, registration system, reminder cadence, breakout-room assignments, and the day-of run-of-show.

"Smooth ops day-of" — debrief

The relaunch outperformed its target by 75% against the worst possible scheduling window — late April, finals season, end-of-year fatigue. The 4.6/5 satisfaction score signals the model itself works; the operational debrief produced a concrete punch-list for cycle two. SLC now has a working template to run SEF again rather than rebuild it.

What the survey & debrief told us
Top request
Move it earlier in the semester
Attendees flagged the April timing as the single biggest friction point. February or early March came up repeatedly as preferred windows.
Format
Engagement was harder in virtual breakouts
Online modality limited the side-conversations and connection-making that make peer-educator events valuable.
Recognition
Give presenters something portable
Strong appetite for a digital certificate or LinkedIn-shareable credential to mark participation as a presenter.
Audience
Open to grad students & orgs
Recruitment ran through program directors. Next cycle should route directly through student organizations and graduate communities.

What I'd do differently next cycle

05 / 05
Timing

Move the date forward

Soft-launching late April hit the worst possible window — finals, EOY burnout, students checked out. Even with that headwind we beat target by 75%. February or early March would meaningfully lift attendance and engagement in cycle two.

Coaching

Build presenter coaching in

Several student presenters were doing a conference talk for the first time. A single 30-minute coaching slot per presenter in late March would lift session engagement — especially in a virtual format where presence is harder to project.

Recognition

Ship a digital certificate

Presenters had nothing portable to add to a LinkedIn or résumé. A simple credential converts a one-day forum into a durable signal — and gives next year's recruitment a tangible incentive to point to.
