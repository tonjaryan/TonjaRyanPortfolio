Lesson
Plan

Build your weekly SI lesson plan step-by-step. Frame your session, design the main activity, plan your opener and closer, pre-write your troubleshooting moves, then review and submit.

STEP A · 2 MIN

Frame your session

The basics. Get this right and the rest of the plan flows from it.

FORMAT: "Students will be able to [verb] [concept] by [method]."
Reach for Bloom's verbs: analyze, compare, predict, explain, apply, evaluate.
OR
← Back to hub
STEP B · 6 MIN

Design your main activity

Break the heart of your session into 2–4 phases. For each phase, write what students will do, which facilitation technique you'll lean on, and your role.

STEP C · 3 MIN

Opener & closer

Bookend your session. Opener should be low-stakes and non-academic — just get the room talking. Closer is your check for understanding before students leave.

STEP D · 2 MIN · OPTIONAL

Educational pain points

Think back to when you took this course. What concepts tripped you up? What do you anticipate might trip up your students this week? Jotting this down primes you for the moments where you'll need to slow down and facilitate carefully.

REMINDER

Before your exam review

Three logistics to lock down before you run the activities below.

  1. 01

    One week out — poll your students

    Send a quick Slido, Slack, or Canvas post asking who's coming. Helps you plan group size and reserve the right room.

  2. 02

    Submit your exam review room request

    Reserve early. Room size and layout dictate which collaboration formats are actually possible.

  3. 03

    Match the activity to the room

    Pairs work anywhere. Group activities need tables or movable chairs. Want students at the boards? Check that you have whiteboard space and working markers before the session starts.
