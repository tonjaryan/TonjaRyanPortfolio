A two-part learning experience for GEOL 1403 (Physical Geology) at Dallas College, designed for future EC-6 educators preparing for the Texas Core Subjects EC-6 (391) certification. Pairs a printable companion workbook with an interactive web module that mirrors the same architecture module-by-module: metacognitive moment, readings with key questions, EC-6 competency mapping, hands-on activities, quiz wrapper, and wrap-up reflection. Activities include a recreated AMNH Layers of Time stratigraphy game, plate-tectonics comparison work, and lava-lamp convection modeling.

03 Companion Workbook

A digital companion workbook for Dallas College biology students, developed as an iDesign deliverable to accompany the course textbook. Each activity is built around metacognitive prompts that help students monitor how they learn, identify what they understand, and adjust their study strategies between assessments. Designed to translate cognitive-science research on metacognition and self-regulated learning into hands-on practice students will actually complete. The result turns what could be a passive reading experience into an active record of how their understanding evolves across the semester.

Please note: the linked workbook is a pre-final development draft. You'll see placeholders like "icon goes here" marking spots where visual assets get dropped in before final delivery. 04 Cohort Tooling

A live operational dashboard for an SI (Supplemental Instruction) biology cohort, used in every weekly session by peer educators leading study groups. Pairs a 5-step lesson-plan builder that turns peer-educator planning into a repeatable practice with a 118-card strategy library of evidence-based teaching moves accessible on demand. 06 AI-Augmented Build

A capstone learning build that teaches educational leaders how to use Claude to design their own instructional tools. The Pixel Solar System serves as the worked example: an interactive prototype showing how evidence-based, AI-augmented instructional design can move from prompt to usable product. By walking through the build of a working teaching artifact end-to-end, educational leaders complete the program able to specify, prototype, and iterate on instructional tools without needing a developer in the loop. The solar system demonstrates the tool; the process teaches the method.

Please note: while the Pixel Solar System may look elementary at first glance, this build was designed to show how apps and instructional tools can be developed via Claude. The artifact is the worked example; the build process is the point.
